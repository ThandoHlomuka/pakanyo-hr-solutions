How to Perfect Your Training Needs Analysis Process

June 2026 · By Bella Ngwenya

Keeping up with the dynamic landscape of professional development requires mastering the Training Needs Analysis (TNA) process. This guide will take you through a step-by-step process to perfect your TNA, ensuring that your training initiatives align seamlessly with organisational goals and individual needs.

01

Define the Objectives

As an L&D Practitioner, it is important to clearly articulate the purpose of the Training Needs Analysis. You must identify the specific goals and outcomes you aim to achieve through the analysis.

02

Identify Stakeholders

Identify key stakeholders involved in or affected by the training process. Include employees, managers, department heads, and any other relevant parties.

03

Establish Criteria

Define your success criteria. Key performance indicators (KPIs) or specific performance metrics aligned with organisational goals can be included.

04

Collect Background Information

Gather all the relevant information about the organisation's structure, goals, and current performance. Review existing training materials, performance appraisals, and any available data on employee competencies.

05

Conduct Surveys and Interviews

Obtain insights from employees, managers, and other stakeholders by administering surveys or conducting interviews. Discuss perceived skill gaps, challenges, and areas for training.

06

Analyse Job Descriptions

Review job descriptions to identify the skills and competencies required for each role. Identify the skills that employees possess in relation to these requirements.

07

Develop Training Objectives

Clearly define the specific learning objectives for each identified training need. Ensure that the objectives align with the overall goals of the organisation. Consider things like the delivery method, resources required, and strategies to evaluate the effectiveness of the training programmes.

By following these steps, your organisation can conduct a thorough Training Needs Analysis and develop targeted training programmes that contribute to enhanced employee performance and the overall success of the organisation.
